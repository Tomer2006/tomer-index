Tomer index

The Tomer index uses a health pillar that is ½ lifespan (life expectancy at birth) and ½ healthspan (WHO HALE—healthy life expectancy), each turned into a 0–1 index with the same 20–85 goalposts, then averaged. Other pillars: GNI per capita (PPP) and safety from intentional homicides /100k. The table lists economies that have all four inputs (no substitute series). It also includes derived World Bank group rows computed from the same country-level data with population weights: World, regions, admin regions, income levels, lending types, and a few obvious unions such as Low & middle income and IDA & IBRD total. Overall Tomer index: LEIhealth4/9×Income4/9×Safety1/9.

Life expectancy & GNI: World Bank (SP.DYN.LE00.IN, NY.GNP.PCAP.PP.KD). Global series and derived group rows use population weights from SP.POP.TOTL. HALE: WHO GHO WHOSIS_000002 (both sexes). Homicides: VC.IHR.PSRC.P5. LEI = ½·LEI(LE)+½·LEI(HALE). Safety = linear 0–60 homicides/100k → 1–0. Tomer index = LEIhealth4/9×Income4/9×Safety1/9.
